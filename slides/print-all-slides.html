The Frictionless Work Initiative

Strategically using AI to remove friction from work — making laborious tasks easier and increasing productivity across all levels.

What "Frictionless" Means

Friction is:

  • Repetitive tasks that drain energy
  • Context switching between tools
  • Searching for information
  • Manual data entry and formatting
  • Administrative overhead

Frictionless is:

  • AI handles the mundane
  • Information comes to you
  • Reports self-generate
  • Context is always at hand
  • Time for thinking, not typing

FWI turns AI into infrastructure — not a novelty, but a foundational layer that makes everything easier.

34

Rules 1-2: Scope Boundaries

Rule 1: Internal Focus Only

FWI is exclusively for internal operations. Any AI that touches customers goes through the standard IT governance process.

Why This Matters

  • Lower risk for internal experiments
  • Faster iteration and learning
  • Protects customer relationships
  • Clear line of responsibility

Rule 2: Time/Scope Limits

If it takes more than 1-3 days to build, it's not an FWI project. Longer projects go through regular IT queue.

Why This Matters

  • Forces focus on quick wins
  • Prevents scope creep
  • Demonstrates value fast
  • Maintains momentum

Bottom line: Start small, prove value, then expand. Don't boil the ocean.

35

Rules 3-4: Ownership Model

Rule 3: Owner Documentation

The business owner (not IT) is responsible for documenting how the solution works and training their team.

Why This Matters

  • Business understands their needs best
  • Creates internal expertise
  • Reduces IT bottleneck
  • Ensures practical documentation

Rule 4: Owner Accountability

Whoever submits the request is responsible for collecting and reporting productivity metrics.

Why This Matters

  • Real data from real users
  • Skin in the game
  • Proves ROI to leadership
  • Builds case for more investment

The model: Business owns the problem, the solution, and the results. IT enables, not owns.

36

Rules 5-6: Control & Prioritization

Rule 5: Limited Submission Rights

Only department heads and certain grade levels can submit FWI requests. Not open to all employees initially.

Why This Matters

  • Quality over quantity
  • Strategic alignment
  • Prevents request flooding
  • Ensures organizational priority

Rule 6: Centralized Prioritization

Kevin and Claudell review all submissions and decide what gets built and in what order.

Why This Matters

  • Single point of accountability
  • Cross-functional visibility
  • Strategic alignment
  • Resource optimization

Evolution: As we mature, submission rights may expand. Start tight, loosen with experience.
