AI Quick Reference

Key concepts and guidelines at a glance — print or bookmark for easy access

Key Vocabulary

Term What It Means (Plain English)
AI (Artificial Intelligence) Computer systems that can do tasks that normally require human intelligence - like understanding language, recognizing patterns, or making decisions.
Generative AI AI that creates new content (text, images, code) rather than just analyzing existing data. ChatGPT is generative AI.
LLM (Large Language Model) The technology behind ChatGPT and similar tools. It's trained on massive amounts of text and learns to predict/generate human-like responses.
Prompt The instruction or question you give to an AI. Better prompts = better results.
Prompt Engineering The skill of writing effective prompts to get the results you want from AI.
Hallucination When AI confidently makes up information that isn't true. Always verify important facts.
Context Window How much text the AI can "remember" in a conversation. Longer context = can handle bigger documents.
Agent / Agentic AI AI that can take actions and complete multi-step tasks, not just answer questions. The FWI "agents" are small automated helpers.
Token How AI measures text length. Roughly 1 token = 4 characters or 0.75 words. Most tools have token limits.

The CRAFT Framework for Prompts

A simple structure for writing effective prompts:

C - Context

What's the situation?

  • Who's the audience?
  • What's the background?
  • Any constraints?

R - Role

What role should AI play?

  • Expert analyst
  • Technical writer
  • Business advisor

A - Action

What do you need done?

  • Be specific
  • One task at a time
  • Clear deliverable

F - Format

How should output look?

  • Bullet points vs prose
  • Length (short/detailed)
  • Structure (headers, tables)

T - Tone

What voice/style?

  • Formal vs casual
  • Technical vs simple
  • Confident vs tentative

AI Do's and Don'ts

DO

  • Verify important facts - AI can be wrong
  • Iterate - Refine prompts if first result isn't right
  • Be specific - Vague prompts = vague results
  • Give examples - Show what you want
  • Review before sending - Always read AI-generated content
  • Use for first drafts - Then edit to your voice
  • Experiment - Try different tools for different tasks
  • Ask follow-up questions - Dig deeper

DON'T

  • Put in sensitive data - No SSNs, passwords, client data
  • Trust blindly - AI can hallucinate facts
  • Use for final version - Always review and edit
  • Copy-paste without reading - You're responsible for the output
  • Share confidential info - Treat AI like a public conversation
  • Expect perfection - AI is a tool, not a replacement
  • Give up after one try - Refine your prompt
  • Use for legal/medical advice - Get professional help

Common Prompt Starters

Copy and customize these:

Rewrite/Edit

"Rewrite this to be more [concise/formal/clear]..."

Explain

"Explain [topic] as if I'm [new to this/an expert/a customer]..."

Summarize

"Summarize this in [3 bullet points/one paragraph/plain English]..."

Draft

"Draft a [email/memo/response] about [topic] for [audience]..."

Compare

"Compare [option A] and [option B] for [use case]. Show pros and cons..."

Improve

"What's wrong with this [email/process/approach]? How would you improve it?"

Create

"Create a [checklist/template/outline] for [task]..."

Analyze

"Analyze this [data/feedback/situation]. What are the key insights?"

Iterative Prompting

Don't expect perfection on the first try. Use follow-ups:

"Make it shorter"

Reduce length

"Make it more formal"

Adjust tone

"Add more detail to X"

Expand sections

"Give me 3 alternatives"

Get options

"Use bullet points"

Change format

"What am I missing?"

Get perspective

Before You Send (Checklist)

Always review AI-generated content before using it:

  • ☐ Read it completely - Don't skim AI output
  • ☐ Verify facts and numbers - AI can hallucinate
  • ☐ Check for your voice - Does it sound like you?
  • ☐ Remove AI artifacts - Look for generic phrases
  • ☐ Confirm accuracy - Names, dates, specifics
  • ☐ Consider the audience - Is the tone right?
  • ☐ No sensitive data - Nothing confidential included

FWI Quick Reference

What is FWI? The Frictionless Workforce Initiative - using AI to remove friction from work. Small, quickly deployed AI tools that make work easier.

The Three Pillars

1. Training & AI Literacy

Getting everyone comfortable using AI tools in their daily work.

2. Custom Agentic Solutions

Building small AI tools (1-3 day builds) to solve specific friction points.

3. Vendor AI Integration

Leveraging AI features in tools we already pay for (Copilot, etc.).

How to Submit a Friction Point

  1. Identify something that causes friction in your work
  2. Document: What it is, how often, how long it takes
  3. Submit through the intake portal (coming soon)
  4. Your department head reviews
  5. IT evaluates feasibility
  6. If it's a 1-3 day build, it goes into the FWI queue
